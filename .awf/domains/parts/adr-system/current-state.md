## Current state

Decisions live as append-only ADRs under `docs/decisions/`, parsed by `internal/adr` (a shared `internal/frontmatter` parser) into a generated `ACTIVE.md` status index, regenerated by `awf sync` and drift-checked by a regenerate-and-compare path. Append-only protects rationale, not orthography (ADR-0115): an Implemented ADR's arguments, decision items, alternatives, and consequences are never rewritten, but a meaning-preserving punctuation fix to its `# ` heading line is normalization rather than revision, and is permitted with explicit maintainer approval. ADR-0118 widened that carve-out, partially superseding ADR-0115 Decision item 5: retroactive normalization reaches the full authored body of every ADR and plan, not the heading alone, and the whole corpus was swept to plain punctuation in one act (roughly 7200 sites across 182 files, fences included). Two exemptions stand, both named rather than inferred: ADR-0113, the ADR about the em-dash, by maintainer designation; and prose where the glyph is the subject of its own sentence, which punctuating would make false. What licenses a sweep that large over settled rationale is that word preservation is proven rather than promised: a word-stream comparison (alphanumeric runs only, old versus new) must report zero delta, so the risk is a flat punctuation choice, never lost argument. Each ADR declares `domains:`, which drives this doc's generated `## Decisions` index (same regenerate-and-compare mechanism). The workflow chain (brainstorm → ADR → plan → resync → impl → review) is enforced by the `awf-*` skills and reviewer agents. The ADR guide (`README.md`), the skeleton (`template.md`), and a plan-authoring guide (`plans/README.md`) are awf-managed singletons (ADR-0021, ADR-0020), so the `.layout.adrReadme` / `.layout.adrTemplate` / `.layout.plansReadme` references resolve in any adopter project. `ACTIVE.md` always renders (a placeholder index when no ADRs exist) so its document-map link resolves out of the box (ADR-0020, partial-item supersedence of ADR-0005/ADR-0006). A new ADR is scaffolded via `awf new adr "<title>"` (ADR-0042), which computes the next sequential number and copies `template.md` with its marker comments stripped and its date/title filled in, rather than by hand; `awf-proposing-adr` invokes it as the first authoring step. ADR-0103 revives the long-dropped `tags:` and `related:` frontmatter: `internal/adr` now lifts both into `adr.ADR`, `awf check` resolves each ADR's `related:` numbers against the decisions dir (`invariant: adr-related-link-resolved`, the ADR analogue of the pitfall/plan link checks), and the corpus is tagged from a governed vocabulary, the relevance currency a later slice will spend in `awf context`. Supersession is structured and machine-checked (ADR-0120, fully superseding ADR-0031 and overriding ADR-0116 items 2 and 5). Full supersession is a parsed `supersedes: [N]` claim whose three-way symmetry - claim, `Superseded by ADR-NNNN` status flip, scalar `superseded_by:` back-pointer - `awf check` enforces in both directions. Partial supersession is an inline Decision-section token, `supersedes: ADR-NNNN#<item>` for a Decision item or `supersedes-invariant: ADR-NNNN#<slug>` for an invariant, one per overridden anchor, with the kind named by the key; `awf check` validates every ref (the target exists, is not `Proposed`, and owns the cited anchor) and enforces the **back-pointer** ADR-0116 Decision 5 had declined pending evidence: a token into a live target requires the target's `related:` to name the carrier, without which the overridden anchor reads as current guidance. Item anchors stay stable because every ADR's Decision section must be column-0 numbered items sequential from 1, checked regardless of status. One successor cannot both fully and partially supersede the same target; a token whose target was later fully superseded and one live anchor claimed by two live ADRs degrade to advisory notes rather than errors. `ACTIVE.md` renders the full chains and the overridden anchors on live ADRs, and `awf context` annotates surfaced entries with theirs. ADR-0116's append-only reconciliation stands - `status` **and** cross-reference metadata (`superseded_by:`, `related:`) are editable in place, append-only protecting rationale, not bookkeeping - refined by the ADR-0120 item 9 carve-out: a live body's *meaning* is frozen, and a meaning-preserving schema retrofit may migrate its machine-readable encoding, the license under which `awf upgrade`'s generation-10 migration rewrote `retires_invariants:` frontmatter into bookkeeping Decision items corpus-wide. ADR-0120 item 11 commits the corpus retrofit that retires ADR-0116 Decision 6's known non-conformance: freeform supersedes-prose citations gain tokens and the deferred back-pointer edges are backfilled. ADR Decision syntax ignores fenced examples for supersession checks and migrations.
